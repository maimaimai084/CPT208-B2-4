CPT208 XJTLU Student Development Project

Group Portfolio Page - Human-Centered Computing

This project aims to create a gamified and systematic academic progression support system for students at Xi'an Jiaotong-Liverpool University (XJTLU). Addressing issues such as low participation in traditional academic progression lectures, information overload, and heavy AI traces in personal statements, we have transformed the entire academic progression journey from the second to the fourth year into an immersive development challenge.

Project Personas

Persona 1: Zichen (Leo)

Identity: Year 3, Business School (Economics)

GPA: 3.4 / 4.0

Goal: Secure a postgraduate offer while clearing up the confusion caused by information overload.

Pain Points: Struggling to identify suitable universities amidst cluttered information; lack of inspiration for Personal Statements, leading to over-reliance on AI and loss of personal voice.

Motivation: Hopes to clarify school selection logic through gamified interaction and learn how to craft authentic, competitive application documents in an engaging way.

Persona 2: Ruolin

Identity: Year 4, School of Humanities and Social Sciences (BA in Communication)

GPA: 3.6 / 4.0 (Goal: Cross-disciplinary application to CS or Data Science)

Core Goal: To successfully transition into a STEM-related Master’s program from a non-STEM undergraduate background through cross-disciplinary application.

Pain Points: Traditional support channels offer almost no detailed guidance for "non-standard" application paths; faces a high risk of failure due to the extreme opacity of information regarding cross-major prerequisites and admission barriers.

Motivation: Seeks to obtain non-standard information and tailored advice through the system's interactive modules to validate her application strategy and mitigate the risks of rejection.
